Most recent projects

Connotate

Connotate has a long-living application for extraction of data from web-sites. The peculiarity of the app is in the fact that they sell mostly the data itself, as in the service of the extracting the data their customer wants. Therefore, the app was divided into the desktop application for the Connotates employees who record the macroses that will be extracting the data, and the web application for the actual data buyers where they can set up the delivery of the needed data.

The challenge

The problem was that both apps were built long ago by the developers and with very outdated frameworks. Therefore, they are extremely hard to learn (impossible to use without the expert’s help) and any significant change threatens to crush the entire application.

So my goal was to propose the steps needed for increasing the productivity and efficiency of their users, and acquisition of the new audience.

The work process

To begin with I made a brief competitor analysis and interviewed the head of marketing and the product owner, we put together somewhat of a customer journey — a map with all the media channels and the product life cycle as the steps, where I indicated the features they’ve got right, the features that can be improved and the features they’ve missed and their competitors haven’t.

Then I interviewed a few users I’ve been given the access to, and made semi-hypothetical set of personas with unanswered questions marked. From those personas and the use cases from the existing web-site of the company I put together semi-hypothetical set of user stories for the critical user tasks from each persona’s point of view. Afterward I came up with a scenario that would touch most of the user stories, chose persona that was in the middle position between the existing and new audience and recorded a video walk-through with the prototypes of two concepts I made along the way. One of the concepts was closer to the desired goals the customer set, and the other was less drastic by the interface changes while still improving the usability.

The result

Finally, I gathered the presentation with the entire process from the goals setting, risks assessment and personas development to the prototypes and the further process steps.

In the end the customer decided that to implement only for the cosmetical visual revamp to make the appearance of the web service closer to the company site without touching the old code. So, that’s what has been done as a result, and the redesign was postponed for an indefinite term.

Empower

Empower is a project of the OpenText where they hope to combine most of their existing separate applications for the solution designers into one powerful editor. The application is extremely complex in the architecture and had a large team of designers working on it.

The challenge

My part in the project was to develop the web based graphic editor for the business process maps based on the BPMN 2.0, and fit the editor in the interface of the larger application.

The work process

I studied the notation and had long conversations with the experts to understand the routine of people who use it daily. In addition, I made the competitor analysis of the similar editors in the web and in the appstore, as we needed the editor to be accessible from the tablets. In the process the editor interface and the stencils were developed, and then step by step actions such as connecting shapes or changing the shape type in different ways, and finally I made the foundation for the shapes properties, because it was planned to add the analytics and simulation later.

In the end I was helping the rest of the designers team with the different parts of the larger application, such as developing a process of connecting the external services to the system objects (database table, share point library).
